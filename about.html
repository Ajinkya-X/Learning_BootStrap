Royal History of Real Madrid

Real Madrid is one of the most famous football clubs in the world. Real Madrid CF, founded in 1902 as Madrid Football Club, earned its "royal" status in 1920 when King Alfonso XIII granted it the title "Real," adding a crown to its crest and elevating its prestige; over the decades, the club grew into a global powerhouse, especially during the 1950s and 1960s when legends like Alfredo Di Stéfano and Ferenc Puskás led them to five consecutive European Cup titles, while president Santiago Bernabéu transformed the club with the iconic Santiago Bernabéu Stadium; in the modern era, under Florentino Pérez.

Fans of Real Madrid are called Madristas. The club is known for its strong history and great players.

Legacy and Titles🏆:

Real Madrid CF has built an unmatched legacy in world football through its record-breaking success and global influence, winning a record number of UEFA Champions League titles along with numerous La Liga and domestic trophies, the dominance of legends like Cristiano Ronaldo❤️ and Alfredo Di Stéfano to modern stars like Karim Benzema and Luka Modrić.
